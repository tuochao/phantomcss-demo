1 Joy Avenue energy score

Energy use is both a key contributor to household running costs and environmental impact. To help you understand the energy profile and efficiency of your property, we have created an energy score based on its unique characteristics.

Learn more

What is an energy score?

Realestate.com.au together with Tendril have created a unique tool that delivers information on how we use energy at home.

Energy is essential to running a household, yet it's something we don't often think about other than when we're paying our bills. Just like cars are measured on their fuel efficiency, your home can now be measured on its energy efficiency. Not only does it provide you with your home's personal energy score, it also allows you to compare your score with other homes in the area.

Who is Tendril?

Tendril is a market leader in household energy analytics. They're obsessed with behavioural science and all things energy related, and are ready to change the way energy is understood and used.

What does Tendril know about my property?

Tendril collects published information from our site and combines it with data from a number of independent sources. They calculate the energy score by collecting information regarding the property's location, year built, the style and size of the property along with a number of other contributing factors. Of course, there are things that Tendril has to assume about your property that will influence your home’s actual energy use. By providing more detailed information about your home characteristics and usage habits, we will be able to generate a more accurate score.

My energy provider can tell me about energy use. What does Tendril do differently?

Knowing the amount of energy going in and out of your home is definitely useful, and this information may already be available from your energy supplier. However, Tendril is able to provide an independent angle with a scientific view of the energy used in your home, and compares that with homes all across the country.

How does Tendril use property information?

Tendril uses a detailed simulation of how a property uses energy. This simulation is achieved through scientific modelling of your home's energy use. In other words, the smart people at Tendril have built a scientific model so that you can have a better understanding of your home's energy use.

What is the Energy Score?

Think of it like the sticker on the front of a fridge or washing machine when you first buy it. The Energy Score provides you with an indicator of a property's energy efficiency. For example: A home with a high score should have reasonably low energy use compared to a home with a low score that may not be as efficient as it could be with its energy assessed use.

What property data most impacts the Energy Score?

The type of property—whether it's an apartment, townhouse, or standalone home—will have the most impact on the Energy Score. The use of renewable energy through solar panels will also make a noticeable difference. The third biggest influencer is the location, that itself will provide information around the energy consumption based on the weather. How the home is heated and cooled, along with the amount of floor space are also valuable features considered in the model.

How can I improve my energy score?

Performing energy-efficiency upgrades or getting energy-efficiency recommendations from someone who is able to view and inspect your property can help increase your score. Stay tuned to realestate.com.au for information on great ways to keep on top of your home’s energy use.

Will my energy score change?

Your energy score can change over time. Tendril are actively working to improve the model that predicts your energy score. As improvements are made to the model, your score may change. Additionally, as more information is learned about your home's specific build and occupancy characteristics, the energy score will evolve to reflect that new information.
